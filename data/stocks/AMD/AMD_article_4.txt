Advanced Micro Devices Inc. (NASDAQ:AMD) has made significant changes to its executive compensation, as detailed in a recent SEC filing. The semiconductor company's Board of Directors and its Compensation and Leadership Resources Committee have approved pay raises and long-term incentive awards for several top executives.Effective July 1, 2024, AMD's Chair, President, and CEO, Lisa T. Executive Vice President, Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer Jean Hu will have her salary raised from $725,000 to $760,000. Executive Vice President, Chief Commercial Officer Philip Guido will receive a salary bump to $750,000 from $725,000, and Forrest Norrod, Executive Vice President and General Manager of Data Center Solutions, will also have his salary increased to $750,000 from $710,000.In addition to these salary increases, the executives are set to receive equity awards under AMD's 2023 Equity Incentive Plan on August 9, 2024. The target award values for these grants are substantial, with Dr. Su slated to receive $28.68 million, Jean Hu $8 million, Philip Guido $6 million, and Forrest Norrod $7 million.The equity awards will be a mix of performance-based restricted stock units (PRSUs) and time-based stock options or restricted stock units (RSUs), depending on the executive. PRSUs will constitute 75% of Dr. Su's target value, with the remaining 25% in stock options. The other executives will receive a mix of 60% PRSUs, 20% stock options, and 20% RSUs.The number of PRSUs that may be earned will range from 0% to 250% of the target number, based on the company's stock price performance relative to the S&P 500 Index and the company's non-GAAP earnings per share growth. The PRSUs are subject to vesting conditions and performance criteria over a three-year period, with the potential for accelerated vesting in the event of a change of control.Stock options will vest over four years and have a seven-year term, while RSUs will vest annually over four years.
